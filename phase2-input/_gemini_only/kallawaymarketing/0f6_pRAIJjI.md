## Visual frameworks

### Traditional Story Arc vs. Scriptwriting Formula

The video contrasts two different structural plots using line graphs:

#### 1. Traditional Story Arc
* **Shape:** A symmetrical bell-shaped curve.
* **Left-to-Right Nodes:**
  * **Background** (Starts at y-axis base)
  * **Initial Conflict** (Low incline)
  * **Rising Action** (Steep incline)
  * **Climax** (Peak of the curve)
  * **Falling Action** (Steep decline)
  * **Resolution** (Ends flat at the baseline)

```
        Climax
        /    \
Rising Action Falling Action
  /            \
Background     Resolution
```

#### 2. Online Scriptwriting Formula
* **Shape:** A steep, rapid vertical ascent near the Y-axis, followed by an oscillating, wave-like pattern (peaks and shallow valleys) gradually sloping downwards towards the right.
* **Nodes along the path:**
  * **Hook** (Sharp, immediate rise at the very beginning)
  * **Body** (First peak)
  * **Rehook** (Small dip/valley)
  * **Second Body** (Second peak)
  * **Repeat** (Continuous waves of Rehooks and Bodies)
  * **Outro** (Final decline at the far right)

```
  Body     Second Body
  /  \     /  \
 /   Rehook    Repeat...
Hook                    \
                         Outro
```

## Visual storytelling devices

* **Pattern Interrupt Zoom-ins:** Frequent, rapid camera zooms in and out on the speaker’s face to emphasize specific points or transitions.
* **Interactive Document Mockups:** Animated text sheets where paragraph blocks light up, change color, or separate into fragments to visually mimic the structural edits being described.
* **Highlight Trails:** Glowing bubbles or marker strokes that follow along with text on screen as if a reader is tracking them with a finger.
